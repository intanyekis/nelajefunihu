What was WWE originally called?

World Wrestling Entertainment WWE was originally known as WWF but had to change its name

World Wrestling Entertainment hasn’t always been known as WWE. From 1979 to 2002, the wrestling franchise was known as World Wrestling Federation or WWF for short.

How was WWE founded?

WWE’s origins can be traced back as far as 1952 when Roderick James “Jess” McMahon and Toots Mondt created the Capitol Wrestling Corporation Ltd. (CWC), which joined the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA) in 1953. McMahon, who was a successful boxing promoter, began working with Tex Rickard in 1926.

Who is the longest WWE Champion?

Bruno Sammartino The longest reigning champion is Bruno Sammartino, who held the title from May 17, 1963, to January 18, 1971, for a total of 2,803 days (7 years, 8 months, and 1 day); Sammartino also holds the record for longest combined reign at 4,040 days.

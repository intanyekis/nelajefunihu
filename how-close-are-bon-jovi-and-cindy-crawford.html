How Close Are Bon Jovi And Cindy Crawford?

Thanks to #FlashbackFridays, Cindy Crawford just reminded us of her iconic appearance in Bon Jovi's 1994 music video for Please be Home for Christmas. It's probably one of the best Christmas specials of all time, and we're glad Cindy dug her archives for this retro behind the scenes photo.

Just look at how gorgeous they both look here, with their sexy smiles and shiny hair totally stealing the shot. But apart from their undeniable chemistry onscreen, how close are they in real life?

The Steamy Christmas Music Video

The music video was just one of Bon Jovi's many charitable efforts to help the needy. Cindy Crawford happily partnered with him and Best Buddies, a non-profit organization for people with developmental disabilities. From that standpoint, it's simply two celebrities, joining forces for a cause.

However, at that time, people didn't even pay attention to the music video's purpose. Everyone was caught up in the rumors that the two were having an affair. First, they were all over each other in that music video, and they sure looked good.

Behind The Scenes Of The Music Video

The dating rumors weren't only based on how hot the music video was. Word got around that the two were also smooching off-camera. It wasn't a pair to be shipped, though. At that time,  both stars were married.

Cindy Crawford was still married to Richard Gere whom she got divorced from a year later while Bon Jovi has been married to Dorothea Hurley since 1989. There weren't big rumors of an affair, but people couldn't help but make speculations. It was all probably just a part of hyping the music video, but it got people thinking, for sure.

What's Their Relationship Like Today?

The truth is, Bon Jovi's wife was actually on the set of Please be Home for Christmas' music video. She was pregnant with their son Jesse and instantly became friends with Cindy. The supermodel would even joke about borrowing her husband during takes. The three have been good friends for years since. They have plenty of mutual friends and share charitable connections. Totally platonic.
